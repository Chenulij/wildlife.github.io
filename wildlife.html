INTRODUCTION TO SRI LANKAN WILDLIFE

Discover Sri Lanka's Wildlife Wonders

The island of Sri Lanka is home to a diverse array of wildlife, including elephants, leopards, marine life, and a wide variety of birds. It is also one of the best places in the world to witness whales, notably the majestic blue whale. A taste of Sri Lankan wildlife is simple to come by, as 12% of the country is set aside for wildlife conservation. The best places to see animals in their natural habitat are safari parks and sanctuaries, especially in the southern and central regions. Keep a lookout for the rare leopard; observe the elephants in a tank or lagoon as they eat and clean; or stroll silently down the west and south coast beaches where the marine turtles are laying their eggs.

Birds Of Sri Lanka

Birdwatching in Sri Lanka is a delight for birders. The island's isolation and tropical climate are responsible for attracting an incredibly diverse range of birds of more than 400 species.
Almost 200 migrant species descend on the country each year having flown south for the winter, and there are 33 endemic birds in Sri Lanka. For more information, see our page on birdwatching in Sri Lanka.

Mammals Of Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka has 102 terrestrial mammals, 90 indigenous species, 14 endemic, and is extensively distributed due to its diverse climate, vegetation, altitude, and geography.
Tourists often visit national parks to see elephants, the island's iconic elephant, and the elusive leopard at Yala National Park. Other attractions include sloth bears, macaques, and monkeys in Wilpattu and Polonnaruwa.

    Don't miss:
  • The Elephant Gathering at Minneriya National Park (June - September)
  • Otters and Sambar Deer at Horton Plains National Park
  • Seeing a leopard at Yala National Pak

Marine Life Of Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka's oceans are home to a diverse array of animals, including whales, dolphins, dugongs, sting rays, eels, whale sharks, and endangered marine turtles.
Visitors can enjoy whale and dolphin watching expeditions or observe turtle eggs. Red Dot offers wildlife-focused itineraries to highlight the island's attractions and beautiful areas, with sales consultants available to help create the perfect Sri Lanka wildlife holiday.
